# Discovering the Power of Structured AI Prompting
## A Side-by-Side Comparison

### The Challenge
Your institution needs to develop a comprehensive policy for AI use in academic programs. Let's examine how prompt structure dramatically impacts the usefulness of AI assistance.

#### Input Given to AI:
**"Write an AI policy for our college"** | **Structured Input:**

 | **Context:** Mid-sized community college (15,000 students), strong transfer programs, CTE focus, existing academic integrity policy from 2019
 | 
 | **Objective:** Develop implementation-ready guidelines balancing innovation with academic integrity for Fall 2025 rollout
 | 
 | **Style:** Policy document with numbered sections, clear definitions, and specific examples
 | 
 | **Tone:** Authoritative yet accessible, addressing faculty concerns while encouraging exploration
 | 
 | **Audience:** Faculty (varying tech comfort), department chairs, students, accreditors
 | 
 | **Response:** 5-section policy with executive summary, use cases, prohibited activities, assessment guidelines, and implementation timeline

---

### Output Comparison

#### Vague Result:
*"The college should have clear guidelines about AI use. Faculty should be trained on AI tools. Students must cite AI use. Academic integrity is important. More discussions are needed..."*

**Problems:**
- No actionable specifics
- Missing implementation details  
- Ignores institutional context
- Requires complete rewriting
- Wastes leadership time

#### Targeted Result:
*"Policy 5.17: Artificial Intelligence in Academic Programs*

*Section 1: Permitted Uses*
*1.1 Faculty may integrate AI for:*
- *Drafting assignment rubrics (with review)*
- *Creating practice problems in STEM courses*
- *Generating discussion prompts*

*1.2 Students may use AI for:*
- *Brainstorming (with documentation)*
- *Grammar checking in non-English courses*
- *Code debugging in CIS courses with attribution*

*Section 2: Prohibited Activities...*
*Section 3: Assessment Modifications...*
*[Continues with specific, implementable policies]*"

**Benefits:**
- Ready for committee review
- Addresses specific contexts
- Includes implementation steps
- Saves 10+ hours of drafting
- Maintains institutional voice
